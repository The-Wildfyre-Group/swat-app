our founder's story

SWAT is the direct result of one young black man's desire to change the state of police violence that disproportionaly affects young black men in America.

Brandon Anderson is one of many passionate voices that has been directly impacted by police killings. His own personal story is very much at the heart of all that we do.

Our Founder's Story

Mr. Brandon Anderson is motivated by a desire to improve the quality of life for victims of systemic discrimination. He has a record of social justice activism, beginning with his involvement in appealing discharges under the military’s “Don’t Ask, Don’t Tell” policy. It was during his service in the U.S. Army as a satellite engineer that he began using technology as a problem-solving tool. Following the loss of his partner to police violence and realizing his own risk of victimization as a Black man, Brandon recognized an opportunity to integrate his skills with his commitment to the pursuit and attainment of justice. He is responsible for the company’s overall vision and business strategy.

Mr. Anderson is a Smithsonian American Ingenuity Award Nominee, a Georgetown University 1789 Scholar and was named one of 100 Black LGBTQ Leaders to Watch by the National Black Justice Coalition in 2015. He earned his B.A. in Sociology from Georgetown University.

Prior to SWAT, Mr. Anderson founded Black Business Matters, a venture advocating for greater employment and wealth within the African-American community through the support of Black-owned businesses. Previously, he served as Racial Equity Fellow at the Center for the Study of Social Policy in Washington, D.C. and interned at the Center for Social Justice at Georgetown University.

  • 1985

    life

    Brandon is born during the Crack Epidemic to a mother struggling with substance abuse.

  • 1990

    Adopted by Grandparents

    Brandon’s grandparents adopt him. His grandpa, a factory worker and his grandma, a seamstress and sales associate for a rental car company also adopts Brandon’s younger sister and their two cousins.

  • 1999

    Columbine High School Massacre

    Two high school seniors murder 12 students and one teacher before committing suicide at Columbine High School in Columbine, Colorado.. Personal journals of the perpetrators document that they wished their actions to rival the Oklahoma City bombing of 1995.

  • 2000

    Brings Airgun to School

    Being cool is the key to belonging, for Brandon. In 2000, he brings an air gun to school in an effort to be cool. He’s expelled from high school.

  • 2000

    Runaway

    Brandon is kicked out of grandparent’s home and runs away with his best friend

  • 2000

    High School Dropout

    Brandon drops out of high school.

  • 2001

    Love and Friendship

    In 2001, Brandon’s best friend becomes his boyfriend.

  • 2001 - 2003

    Life on the Streets

    Over the next two years, the two would live a homeless life on the streets of Oklahoma City.

  • 2003

    New Beginnings

    In 2003, motivated to shape a future for himself and his partner, Brandon completes his GED and enlists in the U.S. Army where he would serve as an active duty soldier responsible for radio and satellite communications in conflict regions around the world.

  • 2007

    Tragedy Strikes

    In 2007, Brandon’s partner is shot by Oklahoma City police officers. The incident involves him being shot and subsequently placed on life support. His wounds would prove fatal. He passes away within several days of being hospitalized.

  • 2007

    Don’t Ask, Don’t Tell

    As an active duty service member, Brandon is restricted in his ability to take leave outside of extenuating circumstance, requiring full detail. He is forced to reveal his sexuality to his commanding officers and unit in an effort to visit his partner in the hospital and later attend his funeral.

  • 2008

    Dishonorable Discharge, Almost 2008

    In early 2008, Brandon is discharged from the military for admission of his sexual orientation. Brandon would appeal the original dishonorable discharge and be awarded an honorable discharge months later.

  • 2010

    College Life

    In the Spring of 2010, Brandon enrolls at Bellevue Community College (BCC) with a focus on transferring to a top four-year university where he plans to complete an undergraduate degree and pursue a career path of social enterprise.

  • 2011

    College Activism

    Brandon is elected President of Associated Students of BCC and to chair the People of African Descent Caucus (POAD) for the United States Student Association. As President he would successfully lead negotiations for the placement of LGBTQ demographics on statewide admission forms to assist in retention and outreach. As Chair of POAD, he would organize a National Day of Action: Justice for Trayvon Martin, a series of discussions held by 12 statewide student associations to address systemic racial inequality following the killing of unarmed teenager, Trayvon Martin by a neighborhood watch volunteer.

  • 2012

    Georgetown Bound!

    Brandon earns Associate's Degree from Bellevue College and is given a full ride to attend Georgetown University.

  • Aug 2014

    Mike Brown Killed

    Lesley McSpadden and Michael Brown Sr. lose their son Michael Brown Jr. to police violence. He is shot and killed by a Ferguson police officer.

  • Aug 2014

    Ferguson, A Call-To-Action

    Inspired by the call-to-action, Brandon puts college on pause for a few weeks and travels to Ferguson, Missouri to protest killing of Mike Brown. While there, he unites with fellow activists and deepens his desire to truly impact the U.S. American policing system, specifically the disproportionate violence experienced by young black men interacting with police.

  • Aug 2014

    “Facebook Group”

    IBrandon creates Facebook group and holds organizing calls to learn the problems surrounding police abuse, violence, and intimidation. Most members had two concerns: 1) How do we express our concerns to law enforcement and policymakers? 2) Once our concerns are voiced, how do we hold the responsible actors accountable? “The stories shared on my Facebook group were a call-to-action. The were an important catalyst in founding SWAT.” To date, dozens of stories have been shared on the “Facebook Group”

HISTORY & ACHEIVEMENTS

SWAT was founded in 2014. Over the past year the company has won over 7 startup pitch competitions, been featured in major news outlets, invited to tech's largest conference and assembled a team of over 10 dedicated social innovation strategist and technologist.

Mission

SWAT is an early-stage technology startup helping cities police smarter by delivering a police conduct-reporting App and Database of police performance to community residents and policymakers, respectively.
